## Section A — Answer any six Questions (6 × 2 = 12)

1. Each of the following sentences contains one error. Identify the error and rewrite each sentence in its correct form without changing the highlighted words.
   1. She **is going to** reads the instructions carefully.
   2. They **are going to** completed the registration today.
   3. He **is going to** not brings his laptop to school.
   4. We **are going to** organized a cultural event next week.

2. Fill in the blank with the most appropriate tense form of the verb in brackets.
   1. By the time the interview round ends, the candidates ________ (answer) technical questions for nearly two hours.
   2. Next Friday, she ________ (work) on the research proposal for two weeks without a break.

3. Choose the correct option to complete the sentence.
   They have been ________ [waiting / waited] to ________ [hear / here] the final results.

4. Write one sentence using future perfect continuous based on the hint.
   Hint: By the time the interview begins, I / practise / my introduction / for a week.

5. Choose the correct verb form to complete the sentence.
   She ________ [has / have] already ________ [finished / finishing] her assignment for the day.

6. Fill in the blank with the most appropriate question word.
   1. ________ of the two internship offers seems more aligned with your long-term goals?
   2. ________ did the coordinator reject your application, even though you met the requirements?

7. Rearrange the jumbled words to form a correct sentence in future perfect and future perfect continuous.
   1. will / by next week / have been / we / practising / for a month
   2. will / by next week / have / the mentor / signed / the approval form

8. Fill in the blank with the most appropriate tense form of the verb in brackets.
   1. By 10 p.m. tonight, I ________ (revise) all the key case laws for tomorrow's seminar.
   2. By the time the panel arrives, the team ________ (finish) the final rehearsal.

9. Rearrange the following jumbled words to form a single, logically correct sentence.
   1. for / been / hours / three / waiting / they / had
   2. she / exhausted / because / been / had / studying / felt / all / night
   3. raining / been / it / had / when / the / match / started
   4. working / how / had / long / been / there / he / ?

10. Choose the correct option to complete the sentence.
    1. The team ________ [had / is] been practicing their ________ [roll / role] in the play for weeks.
    2. He ________ [had lost / had been losing] his keys, so he ________ [had / has] to wait outside.

## Section B — Answer the following Questions (2 × 4 = 8)

11. Write a paragraph of about 170–200 words describing how you will handle stress during placements. Use future perfect continuous at least 4 times to show steady preparation up to key future moments (use by the time, by + time, for/since).

**OR**

12. Write a story about a marathon runner's victory.
    Hints: Had been training for a year — had suffered a leg injury earlier — the race began — others had dropped out — the finish line.

13. Write a paragraph of about 170–200 words describing how you will prepare for an important seminar. Use future perfect at least 4 times to show what will be completed before specific future points (use markers like by the time, before, by + time).

**OR**

14. Write a paragraph of about 170–200 words in the form of a polite email requesting an extension. Use future perfect continuous at least 3 times to explain ongoing work up to a future point (use by + time, for/since).
