## Section A — Answer any six Questions (6 × 2 = 12)

1. Answer the following:
   - a. Determine the number of odd days in 200 years.
   - b. Determine the number of odd days in 300 years.

2. Assuming that 1st April 2024 is a Monday, find all the dates in April 2024 that fall on a Thursday.

3. A shop sold mobile phones over 4 months:
   - January: 120 phones (40% were Android phones)
   - February: 150 phones (50% were Android phones)
   - March: 180 phones (60% were Android phones)
   - April: 200 phones (55% were Android phones)

   How many Android phones were sold in total across all four months?

4. In a class of 60 students, Ravi is ranked 15th from the top. Another student, Arjun, is ranked 42nd from the top. How many students are there between Ravi and Arjun in the rank list?

5. A company's revenue across 4 years is as follows:
   - Year 1: ₹2,50,000
   - Year 2: ₹3,00,000
   - Year 3: ₹3,75,000
   - Year 4: ₹4,50,000

   What is the percentage increase in revenue from Year 1 to Year 4?

6. Find the missing term in the series: 12, 15, 21, 30, ?, 57

7. A person starts from point P and walks 10 km north. Then he turns 90° right and walks 6 km. After that, he turns 90° right again and walks 10 km. Finally, he turns 90° left and walks 4 km. What is the shortest distance between his final position and point P, and in which direction is he from point P?

8. In a certain code, 2345 is written as MOPU and 1670 is written as RNST. How is 5071 written in that code?

9. In a row of 80 students, Ravi is ranked 22nd from the left. Another student, Arjun, is ranked 15th from the right. How many students are there between Ravi and Arjun?

10. Rahul starts from point A, walks 9 km north to B, then 12 km east to C, then 9 km south to D, and finally 12 km west to A. What total distance did Rahul cover?

## Section B — Answer the following Questions (2 × 4 = 8)

11. If 1st January 2000 was a Saturday, answer the following:
    - a. What day of the week was 1st January 2001?
    - b. How many total odd days are there from the year 2000 to 2003 (including 2000 & 2003)?
    - c. What day of the week was 3rd January 2004?

**OR**

12. In a class of 90 students, Rahul is ranked 24th from the top. The number of students below Rahul is 3 times the number of students above him. Another student Arjun is ranked 18th from the bottom.
    - a. Find the total number of students in the class.
    - b. What is Rahul's rank from the bottom?
    - c. How many students are between Rahul and Arjun?
    - d. If 5 students above Arjun leave the class, what will be Arjun's new rank from the top?

13. Table: Sales of Laptops by Five Brands (in units)

| Brand | Q1 | Q2 | Q3 | Q4 |
|-------|----|----|----|----|
| A | 120 | 150 | 180 | 200 |
| B | 140 | 160 | 200 | 220 |
| C | 100 | 140 | 160 | 210 |
| D | 130 | 170 | 190 | 230 |
| E | 110 | 150 | 170 | 240 |

   - a. Which brand shows the highest percentage growth in sales from Q1 to Q4?
   - b. What is the percentage increase in total sales of all brands from Q1 to Q4?

**OR**

14. Two people, Ravi and Arjun, start from the same point.
    - Ravi walks 12 km East and then 5 km North.
    - Arjun walks 7 km West and then 5 km North.

    - a. What is the distance between Ravi and Arjun?
    - b. In which direction is Ravi with respect to Arjun?
    - c. In which direction is Arjun located from the starting point?
