## Section A — Answer any six Questions (6 × 2 = 12)

1. The sentences given below are in a jumbled order. Read all the sentences carefully and rearrange them in a logical sequence to form a meaningful paragraph.
   1. Her eyes are quite red because of the strain.
   2. She has been preparing for her final medical exams for months.
   3. Recently, she has even been staying up until dawn to finish her revision.
   4. This intense schedule has been taking a toll on her sleep.

2. Write one sentence using future perfect based on the hint.
   Hint: By midnight, they / finish / the final edits to the report.

3. Rearrange the following jumbled words to form a single, logically correct sentence.
   1. dinner / had / already / prepared / he / by / then
   2. been / they / for / had / walking / miles / before / resting
   3. the / had / broken / because / it / fallen / glass
   4. had / been / she / since / studying / 4 PM

4. Choose the correct option from the brackets to complete the passage.

   Passage 1: When I reached the station, the train (a) ___ (left/had left). I was late because I (b) ___ (forgot/had forgotten) to set my alarm the night before.

   Passage 2: By the time the police arrived, the thief (a) ___ (escaped/had escaped). He (b) ___ (had broken/broke) into the house through a window that the owner (c) ___ (had left/left) open.

5. Each of the following sentences contains one error. Identify the error and rewrite each sentence in its correct form.
   1. I have saw that man before.
   2. He has work here since 1990.
   3. They have went to the market already.
   4. Has you ever been to Paris?

6. Fill in the blank with the most appropriate tense form of the verb in brackets.
   1. By next Monday, the coordinator ________ (send) the updated schedule to all departments.
   2. By the time the webinar begins, the team ________ (test) the platform since morning.

7. Fill in the blank with the most appropriate question word.
   1. ________ did the coordinator reject your application, even though you met the requirements?
   2. ________ do you usually submit your assignments — through the LMS or by email?

8. Rewrite the sentence using future perfect continuous.
   By the time the meeting starts, the team will work since morning.

9. Choose the correct option to complete the sentence.
   The athlete ________ [had been training / was training] ________ [until / while] the injury occurred.

10. Choose the correct option from the brackets to complete the passage.
    By the time the guest arrived, Sarah (a) ___ (had been cleaning/has cleaned) the house. She (b) ___ (had finished/finished) the kitchen, but she was tired because she (c) ___ (had been scrubbing/scrubbed) the floors.

## Section B — Answer the following Questions (2 × 4 = 8)

11. Write a report for a history journal about the discovery of a lost ship. Describe the state of the ship and the activities the crew had been engaged in before the ship disappeared.

**OR**

12. Write a Question–Answer dialogue of about 170–200 words. Act as an HR interviewer investigating a project delay. Ask 8–10 questions using correct question verbs (did, were, have, should, can). Label speakers clearly.

13. Write a short historical report on why a specific civilization or empire collapsed. Focus on the factors that had weakened them before the final invasion.

**OR**

14. Write a Question–Answer dialogue of about 170–200 words. Act as a reporter interviewing an organiser after an event disruption. Ask 8–10 questions focusing on correct question verbs (was/were, did, has/have, will, can). Label speakers clearly.
